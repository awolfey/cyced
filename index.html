Youth Sail

All Youth Sail Program instructors are US Sailing trained and use curricula designed by US Sailing. All new sailors are provided with Learn Sailing Right, Beginning Sailing and, when they achieve the appropriate level, with Learning Sailing Right, Intermediate Sailing.

Half-Day Program

For children 8 and 9 years old is intended to give younger children a less intensive taste of sailing with greater individual attention.

Full-Day Program

Six one-week full-day sessions for youths 10 to 17 years old sailing in Optimist Prams and 420s.The class is open to all levels, from beginner to more experienced sailors. All participants, especially new sailors, are strongly encouraged to attend a minimum of two consecutive sessions to reinforce their new skills and make the most of their experience.

Learn-to-Sail

For the first-time to novice sailor who would like to test the waters and acquire or improve their sailing skills.This primarily on-water class covers all the fundamentals one needs to know to become a safe, competent sailor.

For those who already have some sailing experience and would like to take their sailing skills to the next level, we offer this path to a nationally-recognized US Sailing Basic Keelboat Certification.

Seminars

Offered on a variety of topics throughout the year. Come learn about topics including navigation and rules of the road, knot tying, pendant making, anchoring, small powerboat/dinghy operation and safety. Classes are offered both on land and on the water by US Sailing Certified Instructors.

Women Who Sail

Meets every Thursday evening May thru October. Open to CYC members and their guests who would like to come down for fun and fellowship with other awesome women sailors. Women members use their own boats and invite others to join them!

Adult Sailing

For 2022 Chelsea Yacht Club is offering an expanded Adult Sailing Program with various courses and seminars offered throughout the season and year round. Our instructors are US Sailing Certified, certified in both first aid and CPR and carry at least a NASBLA Boating Certification and/or a USCG Captainâ€™s License.

Learn-to-Sail

We kick off the season with a learn-to-sail course in May, followed by additional courses in July and September. The learn-to-sail courses will be held on Friday night and all day Saturday for two consecutive weekends. The courses are open to adult beginning sailors as well as more experienced sailors. This 20-hour class covers:

  • Environmental and Situational Awareness
  • Preparation to Sail
  • Sailing Theory
  • Crew Operations and Skills
  • Leaving the Dock or Mooring
  • Basic Navigation
  • Basic Navigation Rules
  • Overboard Rescue Methods
  • Safety and Emergency Procedures
  • Anchoring Techniques
  • Returning to the Dock or Mooring

The course will use the Basic Keelboat textbook published by the United States Sailing Association. The class will be taught primarily on the water aboard keelboats between 18 and 27 feet length overall. CYC will provide a keelboat which meets all USCG safety and equipment requirements, or you may provide your own sailboat4 for a discount. Students will practice their on-the-water skills to gain confidence in operating their vessels. At the end of the course, qualified students will have the option, for an additional fee, of upgrading their experience to challenge the written and practical tests to qualify for the US Sailing Basic Keelboat Certification. This course has a 4:1 student to instructor ratio.

$500/Person. 1

US Sailing Basic Keelboat Certification Challenge

This 3-hour Basic Keelboat Certification course and examination is conducted on an 18' to 27â€™ day sailing sloop-rigged keelboat with tiller steering. CYC will provide a keelboat which meets all USCG safety and equipment requirements, or you may provide your own with the approval of your instructor. The Basic Keelboat graduate will have successfully demonstrated the ability to responsibly skipper and crew a simple day sailing keelboat in familiar waters in moderate wind and sea conditions. The text, Basic Keelboat, published by the US Sailing Association, is included. The only prerequisite for US Sailing Basic Keelboat Certification is that you must be 18 years of age and able-bodied. This course has a maximum 4:1 student to instructor ratio.

$200/Person.2 Courses offered by appointment with a minimum of 2 candidates.3
